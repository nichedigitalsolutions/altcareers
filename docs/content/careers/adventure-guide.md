# Overview

An Adventure Guide leads individuals or groups on outdoor activities while ensuring their safety, providing instruction and creating enjoyable experiences.

Adventure Guides may specialise in activities such as hiking, kayaking, rafting, mountain biking, climbing, abseiling or ziplining. They combine technical outdoor skills with leadership, risk management and customer service.

Depending on the employer, Adventure Guides may work in nature reserves, national parks, adventure tourism companies, outdoor education centres, resorts or private lodges.

For people who enjoy the outdoors and working with others, this career offers an active alternative to a traditional office job.

---

# A Day in the Life

A typical day starts with checking equipment, weather conditions and safety procedures before meeting guests.

Adventure Guides brief participants on the day's activities, demonstrate techniques, lead the group throughout the experience and monitor everyone's safety.

After each activity, guides inspect equipment, complete reports if necessary and prepare for the next group.

No two days are exactly the same, especially when working in different environments or with changing weather conditions.

# Different Ways to Get There

There is no single route to becoming an Adventure Guide. The best pathway depends on the type of adventure activities you want to lead, your experience, and where you want to work.

---

## Pathway 1: Adventure Guiding Qualification (Recommended)

Best for: School leavers wanting to become professional adventure guides.

Typical route:

High School

↓

Complete an accredited Adventure Guiding qualification

↓

Gain technical activity certifications

↓

Register as a tourist guide where required

↓

Start working for an adventure tourism company

### Advantages

- Industry-recognised training
- Strong focus on safety
- Easier entry into adventure tourism

---

## Pathway 2: Tourism Qualification

Best for: Students interested in tourism and hospitality.

Typical route:

High School

↓

Tourism Diploma or Degree

↓

Adventure activity certifications

↓

Work for an adventure tourism operator

### Advantages

- Broader tourism knowledge
- More career options
- Can move into tourism management later

---

## Pathway 3: Outdoor Experience Route

Best for: People already active in outdoor sports.

Typical route:

Gain experience in hiking, climbing, paddling or mountain biking

↓

Complete instructor certifications

↓

Obtain first aid qualifications

↓

Work as an assistant guide

↓

Become a lead Adventure Guide

### Advantages

- Practical experience
- Flexible entry
- Ideal for career changers

---

## Pathway 4: Employer Training

Some adventure tourism companies recruit enthusiastic candidates and provide on-the-job training alongside formal qualifications and activity-specific certifications.

# Salary

## South Africa

Adventure Guide salaries vary depending on:

- Employer
- Experience
- Activity type
- Province
- Seasonal demand

Entry-level Adventure Guides generally earn modest salaries, while experienced guides working for luxury lodges, specialist operators or international companies can earn considerably more.

Average reported salary:

Approximately **R6,900 per month**, although earnings vary widely and may include tips, accommodation or seasonal bonuses.

## International

Adventure Guides working overseas or for international expedition companies may earn significantly higher salaries depending on their qualifications, certifications and specialist skills.

Many employers also provide:

- Accommodation
- Meals
- Uniforms
- Equipment
- Tips from guests
